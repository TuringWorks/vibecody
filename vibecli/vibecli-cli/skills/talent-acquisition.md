# Talent Acquisition

When working with talent acquisition and recruiting:

1. Write job descriptions with inclusive language, avoiding gendered terms and unnecessary jargon; clearly separate must-have requirements from nice-to-haves to widen the candidate pool.
2. Diversify sourcing strategies across LinkedIn Recruiter, employee referral programs, niche job boards, university partnerships, and industry events to reduce single-channel dependency.
3. Optimize your ATS (Applicant Tracking System) with structured intake forms, automated screening questions, and consistent disposition codes so data remains actionable for reporting.
4. Define screening and shortlisting criteria before reviewing applications, using scorecards tied to the job's core competencies to reduce unconscious bias.
5. Design a multi-stage interview process with clear ownership at each stage: recruiter screen, hiring manager deep-dive, technical or skills assessment, and culture-add panel.
6. Build structured interview rubrics with behavioral and situational questions mapped to competencies; train interviewers on consistent scoring and legal boundaries.
7. Optimize the candidate experience end-to-end: acknowledge applications within 24 hours, set timeline expectations, provide interviewer names in advance, and close the loop with every rejected candidate.
8. Invest in employer branding by showcasing employee stories, company values, and growth opportunities on career pages, social media, and review sites like Glassdoor.
9. Build diversity recruiting pipelines through partnerships with HBCUs, diversity-focused organizations, ERG-supported outreach, and blind resume screening where feasible.
10. Manage offers strategically by benchmarking compensation to market data, preparing for negotiation scenarios, and coordinating approvals to minimize time between verbal and written offers.
11. Conduct background checks consistently and in compliance with FCRA and local ban-the-box laws; communicate the process and timeline transparently to candidates.
12. Track recruitment metrics including time-to-hire, quality-of-hire (90-day retention and manager satisfaction), source effectiveness, cost-per-hire, and offer acceptance rate to continuously improve the funnel.

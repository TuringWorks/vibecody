# Learning and Development

When working with learning and development programs:

1. Conduct a training needs analysis by identifying skill gaps through performance data, manager interviews, employee surveys, and strategic workforce plans before designing any program.
2. Apply instructional design models such as ADDIE (Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, Evaluation) for structured programs or SAM (Successive Approximation Model) for agile, iterative development with rapid prototyping.
3. Select eLearning authoring tools based on project requirements: Articulate Storyline or Rise for interactive courses, Adobe Captivate for software simulations, or open-source tools like H5P for budget-conscious teams.
4. Evaluate and manage LMS platforms by assessing SCORM/xAPI compliance, reporting capabilities, mobile support, integration with HRIS systems, and user experience for both learners and administrators.
5. Design blended learning programs that combine synchronous sessions (instructor-led, virtual classroom) with asynchronous activities (eLearning modules, readings, discussion forums) to accommodate different schedules and learning preferences.
6. Leverage microlearning strategies by breaking content into focused 3-7 minute modules, using spaced repetition for retention, and delivering just-in-time learning through mobile-friendly formats.
7. Build competency frameworks that map skills to roles and career levels, enabling personalized learning paths, succession planning, and clear development conversations between managers and employees.
8. Measure training ROI using the Kirkpatrick model across four levels: reaction (learner satisfaction), learning (knowledge gained), behavior (on-the-job application), and results (business impact metrics like productivity or retention).
9. Design comprehensive onboarding programs spanning the first 90 days that cover role-specific training, cultural integration, buddy systems, milestone check-ins, and early performance expectations.
10. Develop leadership development programs with cohort-based learning, executive coaching, action learning projects, 360-degree assessments, and mentorship pairings tied to succession planning.
11. Ensure compliance training is engaging and effective by using scenario-based learning, real-world case studies, knowledge checks, and regular refresh cycles rather than passive slide-through modules.
12. Implement knowledge management systems that capture institutional expertise through wikis, communities of practice, subject-matter-expert recordings, and searchable content repositories to prevent knowledge loss from turnover.

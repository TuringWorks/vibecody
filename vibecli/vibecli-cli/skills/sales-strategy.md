# Sales Strategy

When working with sales strategy and process optimization:

1. Select a sales methodology that fits your market and buyer complexity: MEDDIC for enterprise qualification rigor, SPIN Selling for consultative discovery, Challenger for insight-led selling, or Sandler for pain-focused qualification with mutual commitment.
2. Manage the sales pipeline with clearly defined stages, entry and exit criteria for each stage, and regular pipeline reviews that focus on deal progression velocity, not just total pipeline value.
3. Build territory and account plans that segment accounts by potential value and strategic fit, allocate resources proportionally, and establish coverage models (named accounts, geographic, vertical) that prevent gaps and overlaps.
4. Develop structured discovery and qualification frameworks that uncover the buyer's business pain, decision-making process, timeline, budget authority, and success metrics before investing in solution design.
5. Create compelling proposals and demos by leading with the customer's problem, mapping your solution to their stated outcomes, quantifying expected ROI, and tailoring the presentation to each stakeholder's priorities.
6. Train the team on objection handling by categorizing common objections (price, timing, competition, status quo), preparing evidence-based responses, and practicing active listening to uncover the real concern behind surface objections.
7. Apply closing techniques appropriate to the deal context: trial closes to test readiness, assumptive closes for well-qualified deals, negotiation frameworks for complex terms, and executive alignment meetings for strategic deals.
8. Develop sales enablement content organized by buyer persona and deal stage: battlecards, case studies, ROI calculators, competitive comparisons, and email templates that reps can quickly customize.
9. Enforce CRM hygiene and data quality by defining required fields at each pipeline stage, automating data capture where possible, and tying CRM accuracy to forecasting accountability and coaching conversations.
10. Track sales metrics that drive action: stage-to-stage conversion rates, average deal cycle length, average contract value (ACV), win rate by segment and rep, and pipeline coverage ratio relative to quota.
11. Align sales and marketing through shared definitions of MQL and SQL, agreed-upon SLAs for lead follow-up timing, joint account planning for target accounts, and unified reporting on pipeline contribution.
12. Execute account-based selling for high-value targets by coordinating personalized outreach across the buying committee, engaging multiple stakeholders simultaneously, and orchestrating touchpoints between sales, marketing, customer success, and executive sponsors.

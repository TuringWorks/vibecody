# Cross-Functional Collaboration

When working across teams and disciplines:

1. Understand each team's goals and constraints — before proposing joint work, learn what the other team is measured on, what their current priorities are, and what blockers they face; alignment starts with empathy.
2. Establish shared vocabulary across disciplines — define key terms explicitly (does "deployment" mean merged to main or running in production?) to prevent miscommunication that wastes days.
3. Run joint planning sessions — bring engineers, designers, PMs, and stakeholders together at the start of a project to align on scope, timelines, and risks rather than planning in silos and merging later.
4. Define clear ownership and handoff points — document who owns each component, where one team's responsibility ends and another's begins, and what "done" looks like at each boundary.
5. Agree on shared metrics and success criteria — if engineering optimizes for latency while product optimizes for engagement, misalignment is guaranteed; pick metrics everyone can rally behind.
6. Set a communication cadence — weekly syncs for ongoing projects, daily standups during critical phases, and async updates in a shared channel keep everyone informed without meeting overload.
7. Resolve cross-team conflicts with a neutral facilitator — when teams disagree on priorities or approach, escalate to a shared manager or principal engineer who can weigh trade-offs objectively.
8. Build empathy through job shadowing — have engineers sit with support for a day, or have PMs shadow on-call rotations; firsthand experience eliminates assumptions about what other teams do.
9. Maintain documentation for cross-team interfaces — API contracts, data schemas, SLAs, and runbooks at team boundaries are living documents that prevent regression and reduce tribal knowledge.
10. Celebrate joint wins publicly — when a cross-functional effort ships, credit every team involved in the all-hands or Slack announcement; shared recognition reinforces future collaboration.
11. Manage dependencies proactively — map inter-team dependencies at sprint planning, flag risks early, and maintain a shared dependency board rather than discovering blockers mid-sprint.
12. Break down silos deliberately — rotate engineers across teams for short stints, host cross-team hackathons, and create shared Slack channels for domains (not just teams) to foster organic collaboration.

# Warehouse and Logistics

When working with warehouse management and logistics:

1. Optimize warehouse layout by designing efficient flow patterns (U-flow, through-flow, L-flow) that minimize travel distance, separating receiving and shipping docks to prevent congestion, positioning high-velocity SKUs near packing stations, ensuring adequate staging areas, maintaining clear aisle widths for equipment, and planning for future capacity growth.
2. Configure Warehouse Management Systems (WMS) by defining location hierarchies (zone, aisle, rack, shelf, bin), setting up item master data with dimensions and handling attributes, establishing receiving and putaway rules, configuring pick strategies (wave, batch, zone, discrete), implementing barcode or RFID scanning workflows, and integrating with ERP and TMS systems.
3. Maintain inventory accuracy through cycle counting programs using ABC analysis (A items counted monthly, B quarterly, C annually), investigating and resolving discrepancies, targeting 99.5%+ accuracy rates, implementing blind counting procedures, analyzing root causes of variance (receiving errors, mispicks, damage), and calibrating perpetual inventory records.
4. Design efficient pick-pack-ship processes by selecting appropriate picking methods (discrete, batch, zone, wave) based on order profiles, implementing pick-to-light or voice-directed picking for high-volume operations, optimizing pack station ergonomics and materials, standardizing packing procedures, and integrating shipping label generation with carrier rate shopping.
5. Establish receiving and putaway procedures by defining appointment scheduling for inbound shipments, implementing dock door assignment logic, creating receiving checklists for quantity and quality verification, designing directed putaway rules based on item velocity, size, and storage requirements, and ensuring timely system updates to maintain inventory visibility.
6. Implement slotting optimization by analyzing order frequency, pick density, and item affinity (items frequently ordered together), assigning fast-movers to ergonomic golden zone positions, grouping related items to reduce travel, reslotting periodically based on demand shifts, and balancing pick face replenishment frequency with accessibility.
7. Deploy warehouse automation technologies including Automated Storage and Retrieval Systems (AS/RS), conveyor and sortation systems, Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMRs), goods-to-person systems, automated packing machines, and robotic palletizers by evaluating ROI, throughput requirements, integration complexity, and maintenance needs before implementation.
8. Manage shipping and carrier operations by negotiating carrier contracts and rates, implementing multi-carrier rate shopping, generating compliant shipping documentation (BOL, packing slips, customs forms), tracking shipments and managing exceptions, analyzing carrier performance (on-time delivery, damage rates), and optimizing parcel versus LTL versus FTL mode selection.
9. Handle reverse logistics and returns by establishing clear return authorization processes, designing efficient returns receiving and inspection workflows, defining disposition rules (restock, refurbish, recycle, dispose), tracking return reasons for product improvement feedback, managing customer refunds and exchanges promptly, and minimizing the cost of returns processing.
10. Ensure warehouse safety compliance with OSHA regulations by conducting regular safety audits, training workers on forklift operation and pedestrian safety, maintaining proper material handling procedures, implementing lockout/tagout protocols, managing hazardous materials storage and handling, tracking incident rates, and fostering a safety-first culture through reporting and recognition programs.
11. Track and optimize warehouse KPIs including order accuracy rate (target 99.9%+), throughput (units per labor hour), on-time shipment rate, dock-to-stock time, inventory turns, space utilization percentage, cost per order shipped, and perfect order rate by establishing baselines, setting improvement targets, and reviewing performance in daily stand-ups and monthly business reviews.
12. Manage third-party logistics (3PL) partnerships by defining clear scope of work and SLAs in contracts, establishing integration requirements for data exchange (EDI, API), conducting regular business reviews with scorecards, auditing billing accuracy against agreed rate cards, planning for peak season capacity, and maintaining contingency options to mitigate single-provider risk.

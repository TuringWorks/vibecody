# Remote and Distributed Team Management

When managing remote, distributed, or hybrid teams:

1. Default to async-first communication by writing things down in shared documents, project trackers, and team wikis; reserve synchronous meetings for discussions that require real-time collaboration, brainstorming, or sensitive conversations.
2. Schedule across timezones by identifying overlapping working hours (typically 3-4 hours), rotating meeting times to share the burden fairly, recording all meetings for those who cannot attend, and posting written summaries with action items.
3. Treat documentation as the single source of truth: decisions, processes, architecture, onboarding guides, and runbooks should all be written, searchable, and maintained; if it is not written down, it does not exist for a distributed team.
4. Invest in virtual team building through regular non-work interactions: virtual coffee chats, game sessions, show-and-tell demos, interest-based Slack channels, and occasional in-person offsites (at least annually) to strengthen relationships.
5. Measure performance by outcomes and deliverables rather than hours worked or online status; set clear weekly or sprint goals, track progress through visible artifacts, and trust people to manage their own time.
6. Combat video call fatigue by making cameras optional for routine meetings, using async video tools like Loom for updates, scheduling meeting-free focus blocks, keeping meetings short (25 or 50 minutes), and questioning whether every meeting is necessary.
7. Onboard remote employees thoroughly with a structured 30-60-90 day plan, an assigned onboarding buddy, pre-scheduled introductions with key team members, access to all tools and documentation on day one, and regular check-ins during the first month.
8. Build an effective remote tools stack: Slack or Teams for real-time chat, Notion or Confluence for documentation, Loom for async video, GitHub or GitLab for code collaboration, Figma for design, and a project tracker the whole team uses consistently.
9. Practice inclusive meeting habits by sharing agendas in advance, using a facilitator to ensure all voices are heard, leveraging chat for questions, polling for decisions, and always providing a way for async participants to contribute before and after the meeting.
10. Build culture across distances through shared rituals (weekly wins, monthly retrospectives, quarterly all-hands), visible recognition of contributions, clearly articulated team values, and leaders who model the async and documentation-first behaviors they expect.
11. Watch for isolation and burnout by checking in on well-being during 1:1s, encouraging boundaries between work and personal time, normalizing taking breaks, offering mental health resources, and being alert to signs like decreased participation or quality.
12. Maintain security for remote workers by enforcing VPN usage for sensitive systems, requiring multi-factor authentication, providing company-managed devices or clear BYOD policies, training on phishing and social engineering, and ensuring home network security basics.

# Entrepreneurship and Startups

When building and scaling a startup:

1. Follow lean startup methodology (build-measure-learn) by treating every product idea as a hypothesis, building the smallest testable version, measuring real user behavior, and learning whether to pivot or persevere.
2. Define your MVP as the minimum lovable product, not just the minimum viable one, delivering enough value and polish that early adopters genuinely want to use it and tell others about it.
3. Practice customer development by getting out of the building, conducting discovery interviews with potential users before writing code, and validating that the problem you are solving is real, frequent, and painful.
4. Identify product-market fit signals through strong retention curves, high NPS scores (40+), organic word-of-mouth growth, and users who would be very disappointed if your product disappeared.
5. Map your business model using the Business Model Canvas, covering value propositions, customer segments, channels, revenue streams, cost structure, key resources, key activities, key partnerships, and customer relationships.
6. Choose a revenue model that fits your market: SaaS (recurring subscriptions), freemium (free tier with paid upgrades), marketplace (transaction fees), usage-based, or hybrid, and validate willingness to pay early.
7. Understand fundraising basics including pitch deck structure (problem, solution, market, traction, team, ask), term sheet terms (valuation, liquidation preferences, board seats), and the difference between pre-seed, seed, and Series A expectations.
8. Consider bootstrapping strategies to maintain control and focus: revenue-funded growth, consulting-to-product transitions, pre-sales, and keeping burn rate low by doing more with less.
9. Track unit economics obsessively: customer acquisition cost (CAC), lifetime value (LTV) with LTV/CAC ratio above 3x, churn rate, payback period, and gross margin to ensure the business model is sustainable at scale.
10. Hire first employees for versatility and culture fit, favoring generalists who thrive in ambiguity over specialists, and structuring equity grants that align long-term incentives with company success.
11. Handle legal basics early: incorporate properly (Delaware C-Corp for VC-track, LLC for bootstrapped), protect intellectual property, use proper contractor and employment agreements, and keep a clean cap table.
12. Distinguish scaling from 0 to 1 (finding product-market fit through iteration and customer intimacy) from scaling 1 to N (building repeatable processes, hiring specialists, and investing in infrastructure for growth).

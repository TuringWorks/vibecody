# Email Marketing

When working with email marketing:

1. Build and segment email lists by collecting subscribers through value-driven opt-ins (lead magnets, exclusive content, discounts), using double opt-in to confirm intent and improve list quality, segmenting by demographics (role, industry, location), behavior (purchase history, email engagement, website activity), and lifecycle stage (new subscriber, active customer, at-risk, lapsed); never purchase email lists as they damage deliverability and violate trust.
2. Optimize subject lines by keeping them under 50 characters for mobile visibility, using A/B testing to compare approaches (question vs statement, urgency vs curiosity, personalized vs generic), including the recipient's name or company when relevant, avoiding spam trigger words (free, guarantee, act now) in excess, testing preheader text as a complement to the subject line, and analyzing open rates by subject line pattern to build a data-driven playbook.
3. Design responsive email templates by using a single-column layout for mobile compatibility, setting a maximum width of 600px, using inline CSS (many clients strip embedded stylesheets), testing across major clients (Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, Yahoo) with tools like Litmus or Email on Acid, supporting dark mode by testing background colors and image transparency, using web-safe fonts with fallbacks, and keeping the primary CTA above the fold.
4. Build automation workflows starting with a welcome series (3-5 emails introducing the brand and delivering the opt-in promise), abandoned cart sequences (reminder at 1 hour, incentive at 24 hours, urgency at 72 hours), re-engagement campaigns for inactive subscribers (60-90 days of no opens), post-purchase follow-ups (review request, cross-sell, replenishment reminder), and event-triggered emails (birthday, anniversary, milestone); map each workflow visually before building.
5. Maintain deliverability by authenticating sending domains with SPF (authorize sending IPs), DKIM (cryptographic signature), and DMARC (policy for unauthenticated mail), warming up new sending domains gradually, maintaining a consistent sending frequency and volume, monitoring bounce rates (keep hard bounces under 2%), promptly processing unsubscribes, using a dedicated sending IP for high-volume senders, and regularly checking blacklists (MXToolbox).
6. Ensure CAN-SPAM and GDPR compliance by including a physical mailing address in every email, providing a clear and functional unsubscribe link (processed within 10 business days for CAN-SPAM, immediately for best practice), honoring opt-out requests promptly, obtaining explicit consent before sending marketing emails to EU residents (GDPR), maintaining records of consent, identifying commercial emails clearly, and never using deceptive subject lines or header information.
7. Personalize and use dynamic content by going beyond first-name insertion to include product recommendations based on browsing or purchase history, location-specific offers, content blocks that change based on segment membership, countdown timers for time-sensitive promotions, and behavioral triggers (viewed product, read article); use merge tags and conditional logic in the ESP to render the right content for each recipient.
8. Track email analytics by monitoring open rate (benchmark 20-25% depending on industry, noting Apple MPP inflation), click-through rate (benchmark 2-5%), conversion rate (tracked with UTM parameters and landing page analytics), unsubscribe rate (keep under 0.5% per send), revenue per email for e-commerce, list growth rate, and deliverability metrics (inbox placement, spam complaints under 0.1%); review trends over time rather than single-send performance.
9. Implement list hygiene and sunset policies by removing hard bounces immediately, flagging soft bounces after 3-5 consecutive failures, running re-engagement campaigns for subscribers with no opens in 90 days, sunsetting (removing) unengaged subscribers after 180 days of inactivity, validating email addresses at the point of collection, periodically running the list through an email verification service, and documenting the hygiene policy for the team.
10. Distinguish transactional from marketing emails by sending transactional messages (order confirmations, password resets, shipping notifications) from a separate sending domain or subdomain, keeping transactional emails free of promotional content (to protect deliverability), ensuring transactional emails are triggered immediately with high reliability, and using a dedicated transactional email service (SendGrid, Postmark, Amazon SES) separate from the marketing ESP.
11. Choose between plain text and HTML emails based on context: use HTML for branded newsletters, product announcements, and visually rich campaigns; use plain text or minimal HTML for personal outreach, sales emails, and executive communications where a "real person" feel increases response rates; always include a plain text version of HTML emails as a fallback; and test which format drives better engagement for each audience segment.
12. Design accessible emails by using semantic HTML (headings, paragraphs, lists), providing alt text for all images, ensuring sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 for text), using a minimum font size of 14px for body text, making CTAs large enough to tap on mobile (44x44px minimum), not relying on images alone to convey information, using descriptive link text (not "click here"), and testing with screen readers to verify the reading order is logical.

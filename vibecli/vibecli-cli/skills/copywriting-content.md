# Copywriting & Content Strategy

When working with copywriting and content:

1. Write headlines using proven formulas: AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action) for sales copy, PAS (Problem, Agitation, Solution) for pain-point-driven content, "How to [achieve desired outcome] without [common objection]" for educational content, numbered lists for articles (odd numbers and specifics perform better: "7 Proven Ways" over "Several Tips"), and question headlines that reflect the reader's internal dialogue; A/B test headlines and track click-through rates to build a data-driven understanding of what resonates.
2. Optimize content for SEO by researching primary and secondary keywords with search volume and intent data (informational, navigational, transactional), placing the primary keyword in the title, H1, first paragraph, and meta description, using semantic variations and related terms naturally throughout the body, structuring content with H2 and H3 subheadings that match search queries, writing meta descriptions under 155 characters that include a call to action, and building internal links to related content on the site.
3. Choose between long-form and short-form content strategically: use long-form (1,500-3,000+ words) for comprehensive guides, pillar pages, and high-competition SEO topics where thoroughness wins rankings; use short-form (300-800 words) for news updates, opinion pieces, and social content; match depth to user intent (someone searching "how to build a deck" wants a complete guide, "deck dimensions" wants a quick answer); and consider content formats beyond text (video, infographics, interactive tools).
4. Build content pillars and topic clusters by identifying 3-5 broad topic areas that align with business expertise and audience needs, creating comprehensive pillar pages for each topic, developing 10-20 cluster articles that link back to the pillar, using consistent internal linking to establish topical authority, updating cluster content regularly to maintain freshness, and mapping keyword opportunities to cluster gaps using a content matrix.
5. Manage editorial calendars by planning content 1-3 months ahead aligned with business goals, seasonal trends, and product launches, assigning ownership and deadlines for each piece, tracking status through stages (ideation, outline, draft, review, edit, publish, promote), balancing evergreen content with timely pieces, scheduling consistent publishing cadence (quality over frequency), and conducting monthly retrospectives to identify what performed well and adjust strategy.
6. Define and maintain tone of voice by documenting the brand's personality attributes (e.g., expert but approachable, bold but respectful), creating a writing style guide with grammar preferences, vocabulary choices, and formatting standards, providing before-and-after examples of on-brand versus off-brand writing, adapting tone to context (formal for whitepapers, conversational for blog posts, concise for UI copy), and training all content creators on the guidelines with periodic calibration sessions.
7. Write effective calls to action by using action verbs that convey value ("Get your free guide" over "Submit"), creating urgency when genuine ("Offer ends Friday" not "Act now!!!"), placing CTAs at natural decision points in the content (not just at the end), making the benefit clear (what the reader gets, not what they do), testing button copy and placement, and ensuring every piece of content has a clear next step that moves the reader toward a business objective.
8. Craft landing page copy by leading with the primary benefit in the headline, addressing the target audience's specific pain point in the subheadline, using social proof early (testimonials, logos, numbers), structuring the page around objections and addressing each one, keeping the form or CTA visible without scrolling, using bullet points for scannable benefit lists, including a single focused CTA (avoid competing actions), and writing in second person ("you") to speak directly to the reader.
9. Write microcopy and UX copy by keeping interface text concise and actionable (button labels, error messages, tooltips), writing error messages that explain what happened and what to do next ("Payment declined. Please check your card number and try again" not "Error 402"), using progressive disclosure (reveal complexity only when needed), maintaining consistent terminology throughout the product, testing copy with real users for comprehension, and following the product's voice guidelines even in the smallest text elements.
10. Repurpose content across channels by extracting key insights from a long-form blog post into a LinkedIn carousel, pulling quotable lines for Twitter/X posts, creating short video summaries for TikTok and Reels, adapting core ideas into email newsletter content, developing infographics from data-heavy articles, recording podcast episodes that expand on written content, and adapting the depth and format to each channel's audience expectations while maintaining consistent messaging.
11. Measure content performance with metrics aligned to goals: traffic (pageviews, unique visitors, organic search traffic) for awareness, engagement (time on page, scroll depth, social shares, comments) for resonance, conversion (email signups, downloads, purchases, form submissions) for business impact, and SEO metrics (keyword rankings, backlinks, domain authority) for organic growth; set benchmarks per content type and review performance monthly to inform future strategy.
12. Integrate AI-assisted writing workflows by using AI tools for research acceleration, outline generation, and first draft creation, always reviewing and editing AI output for accuracy, brand voice compliance, and originality, using AI to generate multiple headline or CTA variations for testing, leveraging AI for content briefs and competitive analysis, maintaining human oversight for final editorial decisions and fact-checking, disclosing AI assistance per organizational policy, and treating AI as a productivity multiplier rather than a replacement for editorial judgment.

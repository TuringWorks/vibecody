# Strategic Thinking

When approaching strategic thinking and long-term planning:

1. Define a clear vision and mission that articulate the desired future state and the organization's purpose, ensuring they are memorable, inspiring, and actionable for every team member.
2. Set OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) that cascade from strategy, with each objective tied to a strategic pillar and each key result being measurable, time-bound, and ambitious yet achievable.
3. Conduct SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) regularly to ground strategy in reality, pairing internal capabilities with external market dynamics.
4. Perform competitive landscape analysis by mapping direct and indirect competitors, identifying differentiation opportunities, and tracking competitor moves without reactive pivoting.
5. Maintain a technology radar (adopt, trial, assess, hold) to systematically evaluate emerging technologies, frameworks, and practices against strategic relevance and organizational readiness.
6. Apply build vs buy decision frameworks by weighing core competency alignment, total cost of ownership, time-to-market, vendor lock-in risk, and long-term maintenance burden.
7. Allocate innovation time using the 70/20/10 model: 70% on core business improvements, 20% on adjacent opportunities, and 10% on transformational or experimental bets.
8. Use scenario planning to prepare for multiple futures by identifying key uncertainties, constructing plausible scenarios, and stress-testing strategies against each outcome.
9. Practice portfolio management by balancing investment across growth initiatives, maintenance work, and technical debt reduction, with clear criteria for starting, continuing, or stopping initiatives.
10. Measure strategic outcomes with leading and lagging indicators, distinguishing between output metrics (features shipped) and outcome metrics (customer impact, revenue growth).
11. Communicate strategy to teams through regular town halls, strategy documents, and visual roadmaps, ensuring every team member understands how their work connects to the north star.
12. Conduct quarterly strategic reviews to assess progress against OKRs, incorporate new learnings, adjust priorities based on market shifts, and celebrate strategic wins.

# Clinical Research

When working with clinical research and clinical trials:

1. Understand clinical trial phases by designing Phase I studies for safety and dosing in healthy volunteers, Phase II for efficacy and side effects in target populations, Phase III for large-scale confirmation against standard of care, and Phase IV for post-market surveillance and long-term safety monitoring.
2. Ensure GCP (Good Clinical Practice) compliance by following ICH E6(R2) guidelines including investigator responsibilities, sponsor obligations, essential document management, protocol adherence, and maintaining subject rights, safety, and data integrity throughout the trial lifecycle.
3. Prepare IRB and ethics committee submissions by compiling the protocol, investigator brochure, informed consent forms, recruitment materials, case report forms, and supporting documents while addressing ethical considerations, risk-benefit analysis, and vulnerable population protections.
4. Design robust study protocols by defining clear objectives, selecting appropriate endpoints (primary, secondary, exploratory), specifying inclusion/exclusion criteria, randomization and blinding methods, sample size calculations, and statistical analysis approaches aligned with regulatory expectations.
5. Develop informed consent processes that ensure voluntary participation, comprehension of study procedures and risks, ongoing consent for protocol amendments, and special considerations for pediatric assent, legally authorized representatives, and non-English-speaking participants.
6. Design data collection instruments and electronic case report forms (eCRFs) by mapping protocol endpoints to data fields, implementing edit checks and validation rules, ensuring CDISC standards compliance (CDASH, SDTM), and integrating with electronic data capture (EDC) systems like Medidata Rave or Oracle InForm.
7. Manage adverse event reporting by classifying events by severity, expectedness, and causality, reporting serious adverse events (SAEs) within regulatory timelines (24 hours for fatal/life-threatening, 15 days for others), and maintaining safety databases with MedDRA coding.
8. Develop statistical analysis plans (SAPs) that define the analysis populations (ITT, per-protocol, safety), primary and sensitivity analyses, missing data handling strategies (LOCF, multiple imputation), multiplicity adjustments, and interim analysis rules with clear stopping boundaries.
9. Prepare regulatory submissions including Investigational New Drug (IND) applications, New Drug Applications (NDA), Biologics License Applications (BLA), and their international equivalents (CTA, MAA) by compiling required modules per CTD format and addressing FDA or EMA feedback.
10. Conduct study monitoring and auditing by performing source data verification, protocol deviation tracking, regulatory document review, drug accountability checks, and risk-based monitoring approaches that focus resources on critical data and processes.
11. Implement pharmacovigilance and safety reporting systems by establishing signal detection methods, maintaining periodic safety update reports (PSURs/PBRERs), managing risk evaluation and mitigation strategies (REMS), and ensuring timely communication of safety findings to regulators and investigators.
12. Manage clinical data through database design, data cleaning and query resolution, medical coding (MedDRA for adverse events, WHO Drug for medications), database lock procedures, and ensuring data quality through standardized review processes and audit trails.

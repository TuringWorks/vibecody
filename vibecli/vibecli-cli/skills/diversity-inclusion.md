# Diversity and Inclusion

When building diverse and inclusive teams and culture:

1. Write inclusive job descriptions using gender-neutral language, focusing on must-have skills over nice-to-haves, avoiding jargon and unnecessary requirements (like specific degree types) that disproportionately filter out underrepresented candidates.
2. Use structured interviews to reduce bias by asking every candidate the same questions in the same order, scoring responses against pre-defined rubrics, and training interviewers to recognize and counteract common biases.
3. Build diverse hiring pipelines by sourcing from HBCUs, bootcamps, community organizations, and professional associations for underrepresented groups, and ensuring diverse slates for every open role.
4. Practice inclusive meeting norms such as round-robin input, written pre-reads, async feedback channels, rotating facilitators, and explicit invitations for quieter participants to share, ensuring all voices are heard.
5. Foster psychological safety so every team member feels safe to speak up, ask questions, admit mistakes, and challenge ideas without fear of punishment or ridicule, recognizing this is the foundation of inclusion.
6. Support Employee Resource Groups (ERGs) with executive sponsorship, dedicated budget, and protected time, empowering communities to drive cultural change, mentorship, and belonging from within.
7. Conduct pay equity audits regularly, analyzing compensation across gender, race, and other dimensions, and correcting disparities promptly with transparent methodology and ongoing monitoring.
8. Use inclusive language in code and documentation by replacing exclusionary terms (master/slave, whitelist/blacklist, sanity check) with neutral alternatives (main/replica, allowlist/denylist, validity check).
9. Treat accessibility as a core dimension of inclusion by designing products and workplaces that work for people with disabilities, following WCAG guidelines, and including people with disabilities in user research.
10. Establish mentorship and sponsorship programs for underrepresented groups, distinguishing between mentorship (advice and guidance) and sponsorship (actively advocating for someone's advancement and visibility).
11. Measure inclusion through belonging surveys, representation metrics at every level, retention and promotion rate parity, and qualitative feedback, tracking trends over time and holding leaders accountable for progress.
12. Promote allyship and bystander intervention by training team members to recognize microaggressions and exclusionary behavior, providing scripts for speaking up in the moment, and creating safe channels for reporting concerns.

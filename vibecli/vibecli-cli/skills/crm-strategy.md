# CRM Strategy

When working with CRM systems and customer relationship management:

1. Approach CRM selection and implementation by defining clear business objectives (increase win rate, reduce churn, improve forecast accuracy), mapping the customer journey from lead to loyal customer, evaluating CRM platforms against your specific workflow requirements, planning for user adoption from day one rather than as an afterthought, starting with core functionality and expanding incrementally, and ensuring executive sponsorship with a named project champion.

2. Implement lead scoring and qualification by defining your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) with firmographic and behavioral attributes, assigning point values to demographic fit (company size, industry, role) and engagement signals (website visits, email opens, content downloads), establishing Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL) and Sales Qualified Lead (SQL) thresholds, implementing lead decay for aging scores, aligning marketing and sales on handoff criteria through a Service Level Agreement (SLA), and reviewing scoring models quarterly based on conversion data.

3. Manage sales pipeline and forecasting by defining clear pipeline stages that reflect your actual sales process (prospecting, discovery, proposal, negotiation, closed), establishing entry and exit criteria for each stage, requiring reps to update deal probability and close dates regularly, using weighted pipeline for forecast calculations, implementing forecast categories (commit, best case, upside, pipeline), conducting weekly pipeline reviews to identify stuck deals, and tracking stage-to-stage conversion rates and velocity metrics.

4. Structure contact and account management by enforcing data standards for contact records (required fields, naming conventions, duplicate prevention), implementing account hierarchies for enterprise customers with multiple subsidiaries, defining account ownership rules and territory assignments, maintaining relationship maps for key stakeholders at strategic accounts, logging all interactions (calls, meetings, emails) against the correct contact and account, and segmenting accounts by tier (strategic, key, standard) with differentiated engagement plans.

5. Configure email integration and tracking by connecting CRM to corporate email (Gmail, Outlook) for automatic activity logging, setting up email templates for common outreach scenarios (prospecting, follow-up, proposal), implementing email tracking to monitor opens and clicks, creating email sequences for automated multi-touch cadences, respecting opt-out preferences and compliance requirements (CAN-SPAM, GDPR), and analyzing email performance metrics to optimize messaging.

6. Build meaningful reports and dashboards by identifying the key metrics for each role (rep dashboard, manager dashboard, executive dashboard), creating pipeline reports showing deals by stage, value, and age, building activity reports tracking calls, meetings, and emails per rep, implementing win/loss analysis reports with reason codes, designing forecast accuracy reports comparing predictions to actuals, and scheduling automated report distribution to stakeholders.

7. Automate workflows to reduce manual effort by creating lead assignment rules that route based on territory, round-robin, or capacity, implementing deal stage automation that triggers tasks and notifications at each transition, building approval workflows for discount requests and contract terms, setting up renewal reminders with automated outreach sequences, creating escalation rules for aging opportunities and overdue tasks, and using workflow audit logs to track automation effectiveness.

8. Maintain data quality and deduplication by implementing validation rules that enforce data standards at point of entry, running regular duplicate detection scans with merge rules for identified duplicates, enriching contact and company data with third-party providers (ZoomInfo, Clearbit), establishing data stewardship roles responsible for ongoing quality, creating a data governance policy that defines field-level ownership, and measuring data quality with completeness and accuracy scorecards.

9. Apply customer segmentation by grouping contacts and accounts based on industry, company size, geography, purchase history, and engagement level, creating targeted lists for personalized marketing campaigns, using RFM analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) for customer value segmentation, implementing lifecycle stages (subscriber, lead, opportunity, customer, evangelist), tailoring communication cadence and content to each segment, and refreshing segments dynamically as customer attributes change.

10. Balance CRM customization versus configuration by using standard fields and objects wherever possible, creating custom fields only when no standard field serves the purpose, limiting custom objects to genuinely unique business entities, following the platform's best practices for page layouts and record types, documenting all customizations with business justification and owner, and assessing each customization's impact on future upgrades and maintenance.

11. Integrate CRM with marketing automation by syncing lead and contact data bidirectionally between CRM and marketing platforms (HubSpot, Marketo, Pardot), mapping lifecycle stages consistently across both systems, passing lead source and campaign attribution data to CRM for ROI analysis, triggering marketing workflows based on CRM events (deal stage changes, renewal dates), implementing closed-loop reporting that connects marketing spend to revenue, and maintaining a single source of truth for contact preferences and consent.

12. Drive mobile CRM adoption by configuring mobile-optimized page layouts with the most critical fields visible, enabling offline access for field sales teams in low-connectivity areas, training reps to log activities immediately after customer interactions, implementing voice-to-text for quick note capture on mobile devices, using mobile push notifications for time-sensitive alerts (deal updates, task reminders), and measuring mobile adoption rates as a leading indicator of overall CRM engagement.

# Interviewing and Hiring

When building and running a hiring process:

1. Use structured interviews with consistent questions — ask every candidate the same core questions in the same order to enable fair comparison; unstructured interviews are poor predictors of job performance.
2. Evaluate with rubric-based scoring to reduce bias — define what a strong, acceptable, and weak answer looks like before the interview begins; score against the rubric, not against other candidates or gut feeling.
3. Facilitate system design interviews effectively — give the candidate a real-world problem, let them drive the whiteboard, ask probing questions to explore depth, and evaluate their trade-off reasoning rather than whether they reach a specific answer.
4. Run coding interviews that reflect real work — allow internet access, use a real IDE, give problems similar to actual team tasks, and evaluate problem-solving approach and communication alongside correctness.
5. Conduct behavioral interviews using the STAR method — ask for a specific Situation, the Task the candidate owned, the Actions they took, and the Result; follow up with "what would you do differently?" to assess self-awareness.
6. Weigh take-home vs live coding tradeoffs honestly — take-homes reduce anxiety and let candidates show their best work but risk time inequity; live coding tests thinking under pressure but disadvantages anxious candidates; consider offering both options.
7. Assemble diverse hiring panels — include interviewers of different backgrounds, seniority levels, and roles to counteract individual biases and evaluate candidates from multiple perspectives.
8. Optimize the candidate experience — respond quickly, set clear expectations about process and timeline, provide preparation materials, give feedback after rejection, and treat every candidate as a future colleague or referral source.
9. Write inclusive job descriptions — avoid unnecessary requirements ("10 years of React" for a mid-level role), use gender-neutral language, list actual day-to-day responsibilities, and state the salary range.
10. Run structured debrief sessions — each interviewer submits written feedback before the debrief meeting to prevent anchoring; discuss evidence against the rubric, not impressions or likeability.
11. Handle offer negotiation with transparency — be upfront about compensation bands, equity structures, and growth opportunities; candidates who feel the process was fair accept offers more often and stay longer.
12. Plan onboarding before the hire starts — pair them with a buddy, prepare a 30-60-90 day plan, schedule introductions with key collaborators, and assign a small first task they can ship in week one to build early momentum.

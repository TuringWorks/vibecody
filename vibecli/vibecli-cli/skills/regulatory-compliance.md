# Regulatory Compliance

When working with regulatory compliance and audit preparation:

1. Implement GDPR requirements by establishing lawful bases for processing (consent, legitimate interest, contractual necessity), appointing a Data Protection Officer where required, conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments for high-risk processing, and maintaining a 72-hour breach notification process to supervisory authorities.
2. Address HIPAA safeguards across all three categories: administrative (risk analysis, workforce training, incident response), physical (facility access controls, workstation security, device disposal), and technical (access controls, audit logs, encryption, integrity controls) for all systems handling Protected Health Information.
3. Establish SOX IT controls covering access management (segregation of duties, periodic access reviews), change management (approval workflows, testing requirements), computer operations (backup, monitoring, incident response), and financial reporting system integrity with documented evidence for auditors.
4. Meet PCI DSS requirements for payment card data by maintaining a secure network, protecting stored cardholder data with encryption, implementing vulnerability management, enforcing strong access controls, monitoring and testing networks regularly, and maintaining an information security policy.
5. Comply with CCPA/CPRA consumer privacy rights including the right to know, delete, correct, and opt out of sale or sharing of personal information; implement do-not-sell mechanisms, provide required privacy notices, and honor consumer requests within statutory timelines.
6. Design a compliance program with executive sponsorship, a dedicated compliance team or function, clear policies and procedures, regular risk assessments, employee training, monitoring and testing, investigation and remediation processes, and board-level reporting.
7. Prepare for internal and external audits by maintaining an evidence library organized by control objective, conducting pre-audit readiness assessments, assigning control owners, remediating gaps before audit fieldwork, and establishing a cadence for evidence collection rather than scrambling at audit time.
8. Conduct risk assessments using established methodologies (NIST RMF, ISO 27005, FAIR) that identify assets, threats, vulnerabilities, and existing controls to produce a risk register with rated findings and prioritized treatment plans.
9. Document policies and procedures at multiple levels: enterprise policies setting direction, standards defining specific requirements, procedures providing step-by-step implementation guidance, and guidelines offering recommended practices; review and update annually or when significant changes occur.
10. Implement training and awareness programs tailored by role: general security awareness for all employees, specialized training for developers (secure coding), administrators (hardening), and executives (risk governance), with completion tracking and periodic refresher requirements.
11. Manage third-party compliance through vendor risk assessments, contractual security requirements, right-to-audit clauses, SOC 2 or ISO 27001 report reviews, and ongoing monitoring of vendor security posture proportional to data access and criticality.
12. Establish a regulatory change management process that monitors new and evolving regulations across all operating jurisdictions, assesses impact on current controls and processes, assigns remediation ownership, tracks implementation to deadlines, and reports status to leadership.

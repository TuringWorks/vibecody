# Nonprofit Management

When working with nonprofit management and NGO operations:

1. Develop mission-driven strategic plans by engaging stakeholders (board, staff, beneficiaries, donors) in defining vision, mission, and values, setting measurable 3-5 year goals aligned with community needs, conducting environmental scans, and creating implementation roadmaps with accountability structures and annual review cycles.
2. Execute diversified fundraising strategies across individual giving (annual fund, major gifts, planned giving), corporate partnerships (sponsorships, cause marketing, workplace giving), foundation grants, special events (galas, peer-to-peer campaigns), and earned revenue to reduce dependence on any single funding source.
3. Write compelling grant proposals by researching funder priorities and guidelines, crafting a clear problem statement with supporting data, describing the program model with a logic model or theory of change, presenting a realistic budget with narrative justification, and managing reporting requirements post-award.
4. Cultivate and steward donors by implementing moves management systems, segmenting donors by giving level and engagement, personalizing acknowledgment within 48 hours, providing impact reports, creating meaningful recognition opportunities, and building a planned giving pipeline for legacy commitments.
5. Strengthen board governance by recruiting members with diverse skills and perspectives, providing comprehensive orientation and ongoing education, establishing clear roles and expectations (give/get policies), conducting regular board self-assessments, maintaining proper fiduciary oversight, and ensuring compliance with bylaws and state nonprofit law.
6. Recruit, train, and manage volunteers by defining clear roles and position descriptions, implementing screening and onboarding processes, providing meaningful training and supervision, tracking hours and impact, recognizing contributions, and ensuring volunteer activities align with organizational risk management policies.
7. Design and implement program evaluation and impact measurement by defining outcomes and indicators using logic models, collecting baseline and ongoing data, employing appropriate evaluation methods (formative, summative, developmental), using results for program improvement, and communicating impact to funders and stakeholders.
8. Manage nonprofit finances using fund accounting principles by maintaining restricted and unrestricted fund tracking, preparing functional expense allocations, monitoring cash flow and reserves, developing realistic budgets with board approval, conducting annual audits, and ensuring compliance with donor restrictions and grant requirements.
9. Ensure regulatory compliance by filing IRS Form 990 accurately and on time, maintaining state charitable registrations, adhering to lobbying and political activity limitations, managing unrelated business income tax (UBIT), retaining proper records, and staying current with nonprofit legal requirements and governance best practices.
10. Develop marketing and communications strategies by creating consistent brand messaging aligned with mission, leveraging storytelling to convey impact, managing multichannel communications (email, social media, print, website), building media relationships, and measuring engagement to optimize outreach effectiveness.
11. Build community partnerships by identifying aligned organizations, negotiating MOUs and collaboration agreements, participating in coalitions and collective impact initiatives, sharing resources and expertise, co-designing programs with beneficiary communities, and measuring partnership outcomes against shared goals.
12. Plan for organizational sustainability through succession planning by documenting institutional knowledge, cross-training staff on critical functions, developing internal leadership pipelines, creating emergency succession plans for key positions, and building organizational culture that transcends individual leaders.

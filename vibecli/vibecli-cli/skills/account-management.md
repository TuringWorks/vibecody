# Account Management

When working with account management and client relationships:

1. Identify and tier key accounts using criteria such as current revenue, growth potential, strategic value, and logo importance; allocate dedicated account management resources to top-tier accounts and scaled models for lower tiers.
2. Build comprehensive account plans that document the customer's business objectives, organizational structure, decision-makers, competitive landscape, current product adoption, and a roadmap for deepening the partnership over 12-24 months.
3. Map and build relationships at multiple levels within the account including executive sponsors, day-to-day champions, technical influencers, procurement contacts, and end users to reduce single-threaded risk.
4. Conduct Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs) that go beyond usage reporting to demonstrate business outcomes delivered, align on upcoming priorities, surface risks early, and present a forward-looking success plan with mutually agreed milestones.
5. Identify expansion opportunities through regular discovery conversations about evolving business needs, product roadmap alignment, whitespace analysis of unused features or additional business units, and proactive upsell and cross-sell recommendations.
6. Manage renewals proactively by starting the renewal process 90-120 days before contract expiration, confirming value realization, addressing outstanding issues, and negotiating multi-year terms when appropriate to reduce churn risk.
7. Cultivate customer advocacy by identifying satisfied stakeholders willing to serve as references, participate in case studies, speak at events, or join advisory boards, and recognizing their contributions through exclusive benefits.
8. Identify and mitigate account risks early by monitoring health indicators such as declining usage, unresolved support tickets, stakeholder turnover, competitive activity, and budget pressures, and escalating with documented action plans.
9. Maintain executive sponsor alignment by facilitating regular touchpoints between your leadership and the customer's executives, ensuring strategic alignment beyond the operational relationship, and providing an escalation path for critical issues.
10. Develop competitive defense strategies by deeply understanding the customer's evaluation criteria, maintaining awareness of competitor positioning and pricing, reinforcing your unique value, and building switching costs through integration depth and workflow embedding.
11. Coordinate the account team effectively by running internal account reviews with sales, customer success, support, product, and professional services to ensure consistent communication, shared context, and unified action plans.
12. Measure account health through a composite score incorporating product adoption metrics, NPS or CSAT feedback, support ticket trends, engagement frequency, executive relationship strength, and contract growth trajectory.

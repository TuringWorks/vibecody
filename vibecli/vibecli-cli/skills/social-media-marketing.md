# Social Media Marketing

When working with social media marketing:

1. Develop platform-specific strategies by focusing LinkedIn on thought leadership and B2B lead generation (long-form posts, carousels, articles), Instagram on visual storytelling and brand lifestyle (Reels, Stories, curated grid), TikTok on authentic short-form entertainment and trends (sounds, stitches, duets), X/Twitter on real-time conversation and industry commentary, and adapting content format, length, and tone to each platform's culture rather than cross-posting identical content.
2. Plan content calendars by mapping content to business objectives and campaigns, maintaining a mix of content types (educational 40%, entertaining 20%, promotional 20%, community 20%), scheduling posts at optimal times based on audience analytics, batching content creation weekly or biweekly, leaving room for reactive and trending content, using project management tools (Notion, Asana, Trello) for approval workflows, and planning 2-4 weeks ahead while remaining flexible.
3. Practice community management by responding to comments and messages within 2-4 hours during business hours, using a consistent brand voice in all interactions, escalating complaints to appropriate teams with documented processes, proactively engaging with followers' content, creating saved replies for frequently asked questions while personalizing each response, and tracking community sentiment to identify emerging issues or opportunities.
4. Implement social listening and sentiment analysis by monitoring brand mentions, competitor mentions, and industry keywords using tools (Brandwatch, Sprout Social, Mention), categorizing mentions by sentiment (positive, neutral, negative) and topic, identifying trending conversations to join authentically, tracking share of voice versus competitors, surfacing customer feedback for product and marketing teams, and setting up real-time alerts for potential crises.
5. Manage influencer collaborations by identifying creators whose audience aligns with the target market, vetting engagement rates and audience authenticity (watch for fake followers), providing clear briefs with key messages while allowing creative freedom, establishing deliverables and timelines in written agreements, ensuring FTC disclosure compliance (clear #ad or #sponsored), measuring campaign ROI through unique tracking links and codes, and building long-term ambassador relationships over one-off posts.
6. Run paid social advertising by defining clear campaign objectives (awareness, consideration, conversion), building custom and lookalike audiences from CRM data and pixel tracking, creating multiple ad variants for A/B testing (creative, copy, CTA, audience), setting budgets with daily and lifetime caps, monitoring cost per result (CPM, CPC, CPA) daily during the first week, pausing underperformers and scaling winners, and retargeting engaged users who did not convert.
7. Track analytics and KPIs with a measurement framework: reach and impressions for brand awareness, engagement rate (likes, comments, shares, saves divided by reach) for content resonance, click-through rate for traffic generation, conversion rate and cost per acquisition for business impact, follower growth rate for audience building, and video completion rate for content effectiveness; report monthly with clear trend lines and actionable insights.
8. Prepare a crisis management plan for social media by defining crisis tiers (minor complaint vs brand-threatening event), establishing a response chain of command, creating pre-approved holding statements, setting response time targets (acknowledge within 1 hour for serious issues), moving conversations to private channels when appropriate, documenting all actions taken, conducting post-crisis reviews, and training team members on the plan quarterly.
9. Leverage user-generated content by creating branded hashtags that encourage participation, running contests and challenges with clear rules and prizes, requesting permission and crediting creators when reposting, featuring UGC in Stories, feeds, and ads (with permission), building a content library from customer submissions, and integrating UGC into the website and email marketing for social proof.
10. Develop a hashtag strategy by using a mix of branded hashtags (unique to the company), community hashtags (niche audience conversations), and trending hashtags (timely relevance), researching hashtag volume and competition (aim for mid-range rather than most popular), limiting to 3-5 strategic hashtags per post on most platforms (up to 30 on Instagram but quality over quantity), tracking which hashtags drive the most discovery, and avoiding banned or overused spam hashtags.
11. Build employee advocacy programs by selecting and training willing brand ambassadors from the employee base, providing shareable content and suggested copy (not mandating verbatim posting), gamifying participation with recognition and rewards, measuring employee content reach and engagement versus brand channels, providing social media guidelines and training, and demonstrating how personal branding benefits the employee alongside the company.
12. Enable social commerce by setting up native shopping features (Instagram Shop, TikTok Shop, Facebook Marketplace), tagging products in organic and paid posts, creating shoppable livestreams and Stories, optimizing product catalogs with compelling images and descriptions, streamlining the checkout process (minimize steps from discovery to purchase), tracking conversion attribution across social touchpoints, and A/B testing product presentation formats to maximize sales.

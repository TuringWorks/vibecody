# Compensation and Benefits

When working with compensation and benefits programs:

1. Conduct job evaluation and grading using point-factor methods, market pricing, or hybrid approaches to establish internal equity and create a defensible job architecture with clear leveling criteria.
2. Benchmark salaries against market data from surveys (Radford, Mercer, Payscale, Levels.fyi) matched by job content rather than title alone, adjusting for geography, company size, and industry vertical.
3. Design pay structures with defined salary ranges and bands that include minimum, midpoint, and maximum values, with typical range spreads of 40-60% for professional roles and clear guidelines for placement within range.
4. Structure equity compensation programs including stock options (ISOs and NSOs), RSUs, and ESPP plans with vesting schedules, exercise windows, and tax implications clearly communicated to employees.
5. Develop variable pay programs such as annual bonuses tied to individual and company performance, sales commissions with quota and accelerator structures, and profit-sharing plans with transparent formulas.
6. Design benefits packages that balance cost management with employee value: medical plan tiers, dental and vision, 401(k) match structures, HSA/FSA options, wellness programs, EAP, and emerging benefits like fertility coverage and student loan assistance.
7. Create total rewards statements that quantify the full value of compensation, benefits, equity, retirement contributions, and perks so employees understand their complete package beyond base salary.
8. Conduct pay equity analyses by comparing compensation across gender, race, and other protected categories using regression models; develop remediation plans and ongoing monitoring to close unexplained gaps.
9. Ensure compliance with FLSA overtime and exemption rules, state and local pay transparency laws (salary range disclosure), equal pay regulations, and reporting requirements like EEO-1 Component 2.
10. Manage international compensation by understanding local statutory benefits, social contributions, tax equalization for expatriates, and cost-of-living adjustments while maintaining internal equity across geographies.
11. Design executive compensation packages including base salary, short-term incentives, long-term incentives (performance shares, phantom equity), deferred compensation, severance, and change-in-control provisions aligned with shareholder interests.
12. Communicate compensation philosophy and decisions transparently through manager training on pay conversations, published salary ranges where required, clear promotion and merit increase guidelines, and regular total rewards education sessions.

# Journalism and Media

When working with journalism and news media:

1. Write news stories using the inverted pyramid structure by placing the most newsworthy information (the 5 Ws: who, what, when, where, why, and how) in the lead paragraph, organizing supporting details in descending order of importance, using short paragraphs (1-3 sentences), and writing clear, jargon-free prose accessible to a general audience.
2. Conduct investigative reporting by developing story hypotheses, identifying and cultivating sources (on and off the record), filing public records requests, analyzing documents and data for patterns, corroborating information through multiple independent sources, and building a chronological evidence file that supports the published narrative.
3. Verify sources and fact-check rigorously by confirming claims through primary documents, cross-referencing multiple independent sources, checking statistics against original datasets, verifying quotes with recordings or notes, using reverse image search for visual verification, and consulting subject matter experts to validate technical claims.
4. Master interview techniques by preparing thoroughly with background research, building rapport before asking difficult questions, using open-ended questions to elicit detailed responses, employing active listening and strategic silence, following up on evasive answers, and taking accurate notes or recordings with consent.
5. Follow AP style and editorial standards by maintaining consistent spelling, punctuation, and usage conventions, writing accurate and fair headlines, attributing information properly, using neutral language, avoiding loaded terms, and adhering to the publication's house style guide for formatting, datelines, and corrections policy.
6. Leverage digital journalism tools including content management systems (WordPress, Arc), social media monitoring (TweetDeck, CrowdTangle), verification tools (InVID, Google Reverse Image Search), collaborative editing platforms, analytics dashboards, and mobile reporting kits for field coverage.
7. Practice data journalism by acquiring public datasets (government databases, FOIA requests), cleaning and analyzing data using spreadsheets or programming languages (Python, R), identifying statistically significant findings, creating clear and accurate visualizations (charts, maps, interactive graphics), and ensuring methodological transparency.
8. Produce multimedia storytelling by combining text, photography, video, audio, interactive graphics, and social media elements into cohesive narratives, selecting the medium best suited to each story element, optimizing content for different platforms, and ensuring accessibility through captions and transcripts.
9. Uphold ethical journalism principles as defined by the Society of Professional Journalists (SPJ) code of ethics: seek truth and report it accurately, minimize harm to sources and subjects, act independently from conflicts of interest, and be accountable and transparent about methods, corrections, and editorial decisions.
10. File freedom of information (FOIA) and public records requests by identifying the correct agency and records custodian, writing specific and narrowly tailored requests, understanding exemptions and appeal processes, tracking request status, negotiating fee waivers, and using state open records laws for local and state government documents.
11. Build audience engagement by understanding reader analytics and behavior, writing compelling social media posts that drive traffic, moderating comments and community discussions, developing newsletter strategies, incorporating audience feedback into coverage decisions, and using engagement metrics to inform editorial planning without compromising news judgment.
12. Understand media law and libel by knowing the elements of defamation (publication, identification, defamatory meaning, fault, damages), applying actual malice standards for public figures, understanding fair comment and opinion protections, respecting copyright and fair use, navigating shield laws for source protection, and consulting legal counsel before publishing high-risk stories.

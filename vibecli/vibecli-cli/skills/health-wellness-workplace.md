# Health and Wellness in the Workplace

When working with workplace wellness and burnout prevention:

1. Recognize burnout warning signs early: chronic exhaustion that rest does not fix, cynicism or detachment from work, reduced sense of accomplishment, difficulty concentrating, irritability, and physical symptoms like headaches or insomnia. Address warning signs before they compound.
2. Practice stress management techniques daily: use box breathing (4-4-4-4 counts) for acute stress, try progressive muscle relaxation for physical tension, practice 5-10 minutes of mindfulness meditation, journal to process thoughts, and identify your personal stress triggers to build proactive coping strategies.
3. Set up an ergonomic workspace: position your monitor at arm's length with the top at eye level, keep keyboard and mouse at elbow height, support your lower back with a lumbar cushion or adjustable chair, keep feet flat on the floor, and consider a sit-stand desk for position variety.
4. Incorporate movement throughout the work day: take a 5-minute movement break every hour, use walking meetings for one-on-ones, stretch at your desk between tasks, consider a short midday workout, and aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week overall.
5. Prioritize sleep hygiene for peak performance: maintain a consistent sleep schedule (even on weekends), aim for 7-9 hours, create a dark and cool sleeping environment, avoid screens 60 minutes before bed, limit caffeine after 2 PM, and recognize that sleep debt impairs cognitive function as much as intoxication.
6. Fuel cognitive function with proper nutrition: eat regular meals to maintain blood sugar, stay hydrated (dehydration impairs focus), choose complex carbohydrates over simple sugars for sustained energy, include protein and healthy fats, limit excessive caffeine, and avoid heavy meals before important meetings.
7. Practice digital detox and screen breaks: follow the 20-20-20 rule (every 20 minutes, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds), disable non-essential notifications, establish phone-free times (meals, first hour of morning), and take at least one full day per week with minimal screen time.
8. Access mental health resources and support: know your company's Employee Assistance Program (EAP) benefits, normalize conversations about mental health at work, seek professional help when stress becomes unmanageable, support colleagues by listening without judgment, and advocate for mental health initiatives.
9. Set firm boundaries between work and personal life: define clear working hours and communicate them, create physical separation between work and living spaces (especially when remote), establish end-of-day shutdown rituals, resist checking email outside work hours, and model healthy boundaries for your team.
10. Manage energy across the day, not just time: identify your chronotype (morning lark vs night owl), schedule demanding work during your peak energy window, use low-energy periods for routine tasks, take genuine breaks (not just switching screens), and protect your renewal activities.
11. Build resilience through deliberate practice: develop a growth mindset toward setbacks, maintain strong social connections inside and outside work, cultivate purpose and meaning in your work, practice gratitude regularly, and reframe challenges as learning opportunities rather than threats.
12. Create sustainable work habits for the long term: avoid heroic sprints followed by crashes, build consistent daily routines, take all your allocated vacation days, pursue hobbies and interests outside of work, and remember that sustainable performance over decades matters more than unsustainable intensity over months.

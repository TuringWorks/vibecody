# Market Research

When working with market research and consumer insights:

1. Select appropriate research methodology by matching business questions to qualitative methods (interviews, focus groups, ethnography) for exploratory discovery or quantitative methods (surveys, experiments, analytics) for measurement and validation, using mixed methods when both depth and breadth are needed.
2. Design effective surveys by writing clear, unbiased questions, using appropriate scales (Likert, semantic differential, NPS), maintaining logical flow with skip logic and branching, keeping surveys under 15 minutes, pretesting with pilot respondents, and calculating required sample sizes for statistical significance.
3. Facilitate productive focus groups by recruiting 6-8 participants per session matching target demographics, preparing a structured discussion guide with probing questions, creating a comfortable environment for honest feedback, managing dominant participants, and analyzing themes across multiple sessions.
4. Conduct market sizing using top-down and bottom-up approaches to estimate Total Addressable Market (TAM), Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM), and Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) with clearly documented assumptions, data sources, and sensitivity analysis for different scenarios.
5. Gather competitive intelligence ethically by monitoring competitor websites, press releases, SEC filings, patent databases, job postings, pricing changes, and product launches while using frameworks like Porter's Five Forces and SWOT analysis to synthesize findings into actionable strategic insights.
6. Develop customer segmentation by analyzing demographic, psychographic, behavioral, and needs-based variables, using cluster analysis or latent class analysis to identify distinct segments, sizing each segment, and creating detailed personas that guide product development and marketing strategy.
7. Apply conjoint analysis (choice-based or adaptive) to understand how customers value different product attributes and price points, estimate willingness to pay, simulate market share under different configurations, and optimize product feature bundles for target segments.
8. Design and execute brand tracking studies by measuring unaided and aided awareness, brand consideration, preference, usage, and loyalty over time, tracking brand health metrics against competitors, and correlating brand metrics with marketing spend and campaign activity.
9. Leverage secondary research sources including industry reports (IBISWorld, Euromonitor, Gartner), government data (Census, BLS), trade publications, academic journals, and syndicated data (Nielsen, IRI) to supplement primary research and validate findings cost-effectively.
10. Analyze data and synthesize insights by applying appropriate statistical methods (cross-tabulation, regression, factor analysis), identifying patterns and outliers, triangulating across data sources, distinguishing correlation from causation, and translating findings into clear business recommendations.
11. Present research findings through compelling storytelling by structuring presentations around key business questions, leading with actionable insights rather than methodology, using data visualization best practices, providing executive summaries, and including clear next-step recommendations.
12. Utilize online research panels and tools (Qualtrics, SurveyMonkey, UserTesting, Prolific) by understanding panel quality and representativeness, managing quota sampling, implementing attention checks and quality filters, and choosing platforms appropriate for B2B versus B2C research needs.

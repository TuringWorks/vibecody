# Customer Success

When managing customer success and retention:

1. Optimize the onboarding flow to deliver the first moment of value as quickly as possible, using guided walkthroughs, checklists, and milestone celebrations to reduce time-to-value and prevent early churn.
2. Implement health scoring that combines product usage metrics, engagement signals (logins, feature adoption), support ticket volume, and sentiment data to proactively identify at-risk and thriving accounts.
3. Practice proactive outreach before problems arise by monitoring health scores and usage trends, reaching out to accounts showing declining engagement before they escalate or silently churn.
4. Conduct quarterly business reviews (QBRs) with key accounts to demonstrate realized value, align on upcoming goals, surface unmet needs, and strengthen the executive relationship.
5. Measure NPS (Net Promoter Score) and CSAT (Customer Satisfaction Score) at strategic touchpoints, closing the loop with every detractor and tracking score trends over time rather than fixating on single snapshots.
6. Perform churn analysis by segmenting churned accounts by cohort, size, industry, and usage pattern to identify systemic causes, then build targeted interventions for the highest-impact churn drivers.
7. Drive expansion revenue through upsell (higher tiers, more seats) and cross-sell (complementary products) by identifying expansion-ready accounts based on usage patterns and growth signals.
8. Design support tiers that match customer needs: self-serve resources (docs, community, chatbot) for scale, and premium support (dedicated CSM, SLAs, priority queue) for high-value accounts.
9. Establish feedback loops to the product team by systematically aggregating customer requests, tagging them by theme and frequency, and ensuring the voice of the customer influences the product roadmap.
10. Invest in documentation and knowledge base quality, keeping articles up to date, searchable, and written for the customer's skill level, measuring deflection rate and helpfulness ratings.
11. Build community through user forums, customer advisory boards, user groups, and annual events, creating peer-to-peer connections that increase switching costs and deepen product investment.
12. Handle escalations and crises with a clear playbook: acknowledge the issue immediately, assign a dedicated owner, communicate transparently with timelines, resolve thoroughly, and follow up with a post-mortem and preventive actions.
